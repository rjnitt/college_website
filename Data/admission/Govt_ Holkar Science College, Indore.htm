HONORS COURSES

As Per U.G.C. planning , Honors Courses are available in Physics, Chemistry and Maths subjects.

The outline is as follows:-

Honors Courses start with second year of 3 year degree course. Students admitted in this courses have to take three optional subjects and foundation course but they must offer one additional paper out of honors courses. Those who pass in this honors paper will have to offer the same paper in the final year also. In the third year, they have to take only this honors subject and foundation course and not the two other optional subjects. If the students wish to change over from the honors courses to normal subject group, they can do so in the Second Year, but this change is not permitted in the Third Year. Those students who are admitted into Second Year on the basis of Honors Courses, are not permitted to leave the Honors Courses.

Students having passed in Honors Courses are given additional 10% weightage in P.G. level admission in the same subjects.

Eligibility

  • Students having obtained 60% marks are only admitted in Honors Courses.
  • 2. Admission to Honors Courses in on the basis of merit and not any kind of reservation.

As per M.P. Govt. Directives, reservation is permitted only in general subject groups

RULES REGARDING ATTENDANCE

  • 75% attendance is compulsory for appearing in college / university examination only in special cases like look health cases, N.S.S. N.C.C. camps, sports etc. The principal can give some relaxation. In no condition the attendance must be less than 60%. It the attendance will be than 60% such students of B.Sc. Part one, two, three and P.G. students of Maths will have to appear in the examination as or private candidates provided they have appeared in the internal test. In other P.G. subjects in case of shortage of attendance the students will not be allowed to appear in the examination. 
  • Attendance will be counted on the based of the lectures given in the session. It will be the responsibility of the students to know their attendance.
  • 20% attendance is compulsory for appearing as a private candidate. It the attendance is less than 20% such students will not be allowed to appear in the examination.
