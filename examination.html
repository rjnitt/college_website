Scheme Of Graduate Level Examination

According to autonomous scheme Holkar science college organizes its on examinations. for theory of every subject 30% marks are for internal test and 70% marks are for main examination. The internal test programme is declared from time to time to the students It is compulsory to pass the Theory and practical examination separately. At least 33% marks in theory main examination and in theory main examination and internal test 33% marks jointly are required to declare pass. In practical examination 33% will be pass marks.

The assessment of division :- The division of B.Sc. Will be decided after passing the third year of the B.Sc. For this the marks obtained in B.Sc. Part one B.Sc. Part two and B.Sc. Part three will be jointly considered :-

  • First class 60% or more
  • Second class less than 60% and 45%
  • Third class less than 45%


Internal text is compulsory :-On graduate level there will be two internal tests. First internal test will be from the first three units of the syllabus . Its weightage will be 80% in the second internal test concerned teacher will include the remaining two units for 20% weightage and do the total evaluation of the students. To be eligible for the main examination the student must appear in the first internal test or its retest in all the subjects. The students who remain absent in the first internal test will not be eligible for the main examination. The total obtained marks of the two internal test will be added to main examination on the basis of 30% proportionate weightage..
If a student goes to attend a programme after being nominated by the principal and so in unable take the internal test , or due to some extra ordinary circumstances a student in unable to appear in the inter test. For such students after the principal's order a special internal test can be arranged. For this the student will have to deposit extra fees. The case of extra ordinary circumstances will be decided by a committee set up by the principal.
The internal test marks of ex. Students will remain as before and their main examination with be on the basis of current session syllabus. Such students will have no eligibility to appear in the next session examination as ex- private candidates.

Post Graduate Examination Scheme :

M.Sc. Examination will be arranged by semester system. Two year syllabus is divided in four semesters. Examination will be arranged in each semester for theory and practical syllabus.

In theory examination 20% marks will be for internal test and 80% marks for semester main examination. The method of internal test will be decided by the head of the concerned department. It is expected that the students would understand the system and form of the internal test from their heads of the departments, it will be the responsibility of the students. For appearing in every semester examination regular students will have to appear at least in one complete internal test other wise they will be not eligible for the semester examination. There will be Two internal tests in a semester. It is compulsory to pass theory and practical examinations separately. In practical examination like a theory examination the students will have eligibility to pass and carry.

Minimum passing marks are 36% for second division minimum marks are 48% and for the first class 60% marks are compulsory. The division will be decided on the basis of marks obtained in theory, practical and internal test marks after the fourth semester


To Pass The Examination :-
  • In every question paper minimum 20% and 36% of all the question paper's total marks are compulsory.
  • In semester theory main examination 36% marks must be obtained and joing total of theory and internal test also must be 36%.
  • It a student obtains 36% of the total marks but in some paper gets less than 20% marks such student will not be declared pass. In the paper that he secured less than 20% marks he will have to carry the paper in the partial examination. Such student will be studying in the next semester as a regular student will have to appear in the main as well as in the partial examination compulsorily. Partial examination will be held with next semester examination. If a students is declared eligible to appear in the first semester text then he will have to appear in the partial examination with the theory examination of the second semester programme as declared by the institution. This arrangement will be applicable to the remaining semester examinations.
  • If a student obtains less than 20% marks in two papers he will be declared fail. Such fail student will have to appear as ex-student in the full examination according the declared time table of the examination. He will have to appear in the examination according to the syllabus taught at the time for the semester. It will be the responsibility of the ex-student to know about the current syllabus.
  • If a student get 20% or more marks in every theory question papers is 36% but total of internal and theory marks is less than 36% the student will be declared as fail . Such students will be eligible to appear in the next session examination as ex-students. The internal test marks of such student will be as before.
  • If a student gets 20% or more marks in every Theory question paper and the total marks of theory question paper is 36% but the total of internal and theory marks is less than 36% the student will be declared as tail such students will be eligible to appear in the next session on as ex-students . The internal text marks of the student will be as before.

Some Other Points
  • In the degree and postgraduate main examinations maximum two answer books can be re-valued.
  • Revaluation is not allowed for internal test, practical supplementary, and Carry examinations.
  • In semester scheme no student will be allowed t o carry more than one question paper.
  • No student will be allowed to reappear in full or partial examination to improve marks, percentage or division.
  • Details
    • Three grace marks can be given on graduate level to pass the examination. To improve division one mark can be given as special grace mark. No student can get the advantage of the two kinds of grace marks.
    • On post graduate level one grace mark can be given to pass or improve the division. No student can get the advantage of the two kinds of grace marks.
    • The advantage of grace mark will not be given for deciding the merit order.
  • For the degree or interim degree the competent officer of the D.A.V.V. will have to be contacted.
  • Unfair means and misbehavior are punishable if a student of the college is found using unfair means he will be punished in the following meaner:-
    • Use of unfairmeans in internal test :-
      • If a student is found using unfair means or misbehaving then his internal test of all subjects will be cancelled. If a student is found using unfair means second time in the internal test then his admission in the college will automatically stand cancelled and he will be debarred from the autonomous scheme.
      • If a student is found using unfair means in the main or supplementary examination then the decision of the committee set up for deciding unfair means will decide the case. The decision of the committee will be final and binding.
    • Use of Unfair means in the Main and Supplementary Examination :-
      • if any student is found guitty of use of unfair means in the main or supplementary examination, the decision of the concerned committee will be final and binding on all.
  • Supplementary Examination :- A student will be permitted supplementary examination only in one subject at the graduate level. In case the student fails ever in the supplementary examination or fails in more than one subject in the final examination he will have to appear in all the papers of the same class as an ex-student in the next academic session. Ex-students are required to seek guidance regarding the arrangement of practicals from the concerned head of the department. It is responsibility of the ex-students to obtain information about the dates of the practical examination. For eligibility to appear in the examination, the ex-students must get themselves registered on / before 14 th. August.
